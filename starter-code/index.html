The world of Jazz dances!

The Lindy hop is an American dance which was born in Harlem, New York City, in 1928 and has evolved since then with the jazz music of that time. It was very popular during the swing era of the late 1930s and early 1940s. Lindy was a fusion of many dances that preceded it or were popular during its development. It is characterized by twists, turns, and open-position movement.

The Charleston is a dance named for the harbor city of Charleston, South Carolina. It has a characteristic beat. While the dance probably came from the African - American dance called Juba, it was probably newly devised for popular appeal. At first it involved a simple and rythmical twisting of the feet in a lazy sort of way. When the dance hit Harlem, a new version was added. It became a fast kicking step. Further changes were undoubtedly made before the dance was put on stage.

The Balboa is a swing dance that originated in Southern California during the 1920s (though it may have started as early as 1915) and enjoyed huge popularity during the 1930s and 1940s. The term Balboa originally referred to a dance characterized by its close embrace and full body connection. It emphasizes rhythmic weight shifts and lead-follow partnership. Because of its emphasis on subtlety and partnering rather than flashy tricks, Pure Balboa is considered more of a "dancer's dance" than a "spectator's dance".
